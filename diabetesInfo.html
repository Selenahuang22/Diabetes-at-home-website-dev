What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a disease that occurs when a person's blood sugar
is too high. Blood sugar is people's main energy source, from
the food people eat. Insulin is a hormone produced by the
pancreas, which can help glucose in food enter human cells
for energy. Sometimes, the human body cannot produce
enough or any insulin, or cannot use insulin well. Glucose will
remain in the human blood and will not reach the cells. Over
time, too much glucose in the blood can lead to health
problems. Symptoms include excessive drinking, polyuria,
overeating, weight loss, fatigue, and obesity. Diabetes is mainly
divided into three types: Type 1 diabetes, Type 2 diabetes and
Gestational diabetes. Although diabetes can not be cured,
people can prevent diabetes by controlling diet and exercise.
